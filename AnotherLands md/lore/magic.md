# ✨ Magic

## **1. What Magic Is**

Magic is the world's response to dimensional residue.

When the shifted creatures forced their dimension to overlap with this one,
they left a permanent scar. The invasion ended, the creatures were gone,
but the dimensional bleed never fully closed.

Roughly sixty years after the Collapse, people began noticing
that this residue could be interacted with.
They called it magic. Most of them didn't think much of it after that.

---

## **2. What Magic Does**

Magic is the ability to alter the properties of what already exists.

Not creation. Not summoning. Alteration.

Heat something up. Speed it up. Compress it. Redirect it.
A fireball isn't fire conjured from nothing —
it's existing air, heated rapidly, directed outward.

The elements are already there.
Magic is just the instruction set for changing them.

---

## **3. How It Works**

Runes are the language. Sequences are the logic.

Each rune carries a consistent meaning — an element, a property, a direction.
Combining them writes an instruction the world's residue can execute.

Like programming, the order matters. The combination matters.
A clean sequence produces a clean effect.
A contradictory sequence doesn't fail gracefully —
it resolves the conflict the only way physics knows how.

Usually an explosion.

The upside: once you understand why it exploded,
you can use that property deliberately.
The dangerous interaction becomes the intended one.

---

## **4. How the World Relates to It**

Most people ignore it.

MitTech already works. The residue carries the association
of the worst event in living memory.
And the early experimenters had a habit of dying loudly.

The few who stuck with it long enough to survive
are the ones who actually understand it.
They're looked at as eccentric at best.

Magic isn't a lost ancient art waiting to be rediscovered.
It's an unwanted side effect that turned out to be useful
to the small number of people willing to learn it the hard way.
