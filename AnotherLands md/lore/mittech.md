# 🧤 MitTech — The Integrated Utility Glove

## **1. What MitTech Is**

MitTech is an integrated utility glove — a wearable device that serves as the player's central hub for all UI and survival functions.

Originally invented by a crafter bloodline before the Collapse, MitTech was designed as a **survival tool** — built for people going out into a dangerous world and needing every advantage possible.

That origin has since been buried.

Today, most people know MitTech as a consumer product manufactured and sold by a powerful corporation that controls the world's economy. The company took the invention by force, stripped it down for civilian use, and rewrote the narrative so thoroughly that almost nobody remembers — or questions — where it came from.

---

## **2. The Three Tiers**

### 🔧 Original MitTech *(Lore Only)*
The prototype created by the original inventor. Never made public.
Capabilities so far beyond anything that exists today that it remains a myth — telekinetic force, complete environmental interface, and features no current version can replicate.
It exists only in fragments of history. No surviving unit is known.

### 🖤 Stolen MitTech *(The World's Standard)*
The company's manufactured version. Ubiquitous — essentially the world's smartphone.
A cold, barely-there **wireframe glove**. Lightweight, comfortable, barely felt on the hand.
Functional for everyday civilian life: communication, basic mapping, transactions.
Survival features were deliberately stripped out. A population that can't craft is a population that has to buy.
Nobody questions it because nobody knows there was ever anything better.

### 🤎 Real MitTech *(The Player's Glove)*
The true lineage. Built from the original recipes, passed down through the crafter bloodline.
A **leather glove with a steel skeleton at the back** — visibly older, heavier, more deliberate than anything the company sells.
Projects interfaces, maps, and crafting surfaces through the steel framework.
Functions as a partial **exoskeleton**, reinforcing the hand and giving the wearer significantly stronger grip and physical capability.
To anyone who sees it, it just looks like an antique. Nobody recognizes it for what it is.
The real recipes are embedded in the device itself.

---

## **3. Design Philosophy**

| | Stolen MitTech | Real MitTech |
|---|---|---|
| **Built for** | Civilian everyday use | Survival |
| **Feel** | Weightless, invisible | Present, physical |
| **Appearance** | Sleek wireframe | Leather and steel |
| **Crafting** | None | Full recipe access |
| **Exoskeleton** | No | Yes |
| **Who has it** | Everyone | Only the player |

---

## **4. How Players Receive It**

Players wake up with no memory — no name, no past, no explanation.

Nearby, they find an old worn glove. It feels oddly familiar. They put it on.

In multiplayer, each player finds their own. The bloodline scales naturally — they woke up together, confused together, and each happened to find a glove. The company's problem just got proportionally larger.

---

## **5. Gameplay Functions**

The glove is the player's complete interface with the world:

- **Inventory** management
- **Map** and zone tracking
- **Quest** and note tracking
- **Crafting** access — recipes embedded in the device
- **Drone interface** and accessory modules
- **Exoskeleton** — stronger grip enabling actions Stolen MitTech users physically cannot perform

Functions expand as the player progresses, unlocking new modules through exploration and crafting.

---

## **6. The Exoskeleton — Gameplay Integration** *(To Be Expanded)*

The steel skeleton reinforcing the glove gives the player a meaningful physical edge.
Exact gameplay integration is still being decided, but current directions include:

- Enabling complex crafting recipes that require physical precision
- Unlocking interactions with objects too heavy or sealed for wireframe gloves
- Potential melee or class synergies
- Gating certain loot or secrets behind Real MitTech capability

The underlying logic: **crafting requires the exoskeleton**. The company stripped this out intentionally. The player can do things others simply can't — not because of magic, but because they have the real tool.

---

## **7. What the World Thinks**

Nobody in the world knows Real MitTech exists.

The crafters' village carries the history quietly, but they have no reason to speak up and every reason not to. The company rewrote the story long ago. To anyone the player meets, the glove just looks old — an outdated antique from before the company standardized everything.

That perception is armor. For now.
